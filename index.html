Short Bio

Shiqing received the joint BSc degrees in computer science from China Jiliang University (China) and Auckland University of Technology (New Zealand) in 2016. After that, he pursued an MSc degree at Auckland Univerisity of Technology, and then in April 2018, upgraded to a doctoral candidate. In October 2019, Shiqing transferred his study to the University of Tasmania (Australia). He is currently a doctoral candidate in the Discipline of Information & Communication Technology, in the School of Technology, Environments and Design, at the University of Tasmania.

Shiqing's research involve many topics, including:

  • Incentive Allocation Problem.
  • Influence Diffusion Analysis, and Influence Maximization Problem.
  • Agent-based Modeling, Multi-Agent Systems.
  • Deep Learning (RNN and GNN), Reinforcement Learning (DRL, HRL and MARL).
  • Online Learning, Multi-Armed Bandit.
