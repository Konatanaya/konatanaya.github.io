Short Bio

Shiqing received the joint BSc degrees in computer science from China Jiliang University (China) and Auckland University of Technology (New Zealand) in 2016. After that, he pursued an MSc degree at Auckland Univerisity of Technology, and then in April 2018, upgraded to a doctoral candidate. In October 2019, Shiqing transferred his study to the University of Tasmania (Australia). He is currently a doctoral candidate, supervised by A/Prof. Quan Bai, in the Discipline of Information & Communication Technology, the School of Technology, Environments and Design, at the University of Tasmania.

Shiqing's research interests involve many topics, including:

  • Incentive Allocation Problem.
  • Influence Diffusion Analysis and Influence Maximization Problem.
  • Agent-based Modeling and Multi-Agent Systems.
  • Deep Learning (RNN and GNN) and Reinforcement Learning (DRL, HRL, and MARL).
